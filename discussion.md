# Discussion

This page presents a discussion of the rebuttal table approach, including its benefits, limitations, and best practices for future use.

## Benefits

- **Clarity** – Structured tables make it easy for editors and reviewers to verify that every comment has been addressed.
- **Transparency** – Documenting manuscript changes alongside responses helps establish trust with the editorial team.
- **Efficiency** – A standardized format reduces the time authors spend organizing their rebuttals.
- **Traceability** – Numbered comments and explicit page/line references allow reviewers to quickly locate changes in the revised manuscript.

## Limitations

- Rebuttal tables may become unwieldy for papers with a very large number of reviewer comments.
- The format assumes a single round of review; iterative reviews may require additional tracking mechanisms.

## Best Practices

1. Start populating the rebuttal table as soon as reviewer comments are received.
2. Keep responses concise—aim for clarity over length.
3. Use consistent formatting throughout the table.
4. Have a co-author review the completed table before submission.

## Conclusion

Rebuttal tables are a simple yet powerful tool for managing the peer-review response process. By providing a clear structure for addressing reviewer concerns, they help authors communicate their revisions effectively and improve the overall quality of the manuscript.
